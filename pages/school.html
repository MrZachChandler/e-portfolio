Nursing Check In

Overview

Think of the Auburn Nursing Check-In app as a virtual time clock and each students' device a digital punch card. The students will check in when they arrive to clinical assignments and check out when they leave to verify attendance and log their hours. They can refer to their punch cards to see how much time they have accumulated at different sites and evaluate the staff for each day they attend. The persons responsible for the students at the sites (we'll call them facilitators) can later review the students' digital punch-cards and fill out evaluations on them. At the end of the program, the teachers (we'll call them administrators) can review the students' and facilitators' performance and report results to the University so the students can graduate and the system can provide better quality assurance of the sites and facilitators. We are built a functioning Web Client, Android Application, and iOS Application. We used the Parse Mean Stack as our backend service.

UDP Client and Server

Overview

The purpose of this assignment is to implement a simple Web service over the UDP transport service. You will write the Web client and server programs that will communicate over the College of Engineering LAN. You will also learn other important functions in computer networks: (1) implementation of segmentation and re-assembly of long messages, (2) detecting errors in the received packets, and (3) emulation of packet errors generation and detection.segments that will fit into a packet of size allowable by the network. Each segment is then placed into a 128-byte packet that is allowed by the network. The packet must contain a header that contains information for error detection and other protocol information. You may design your own header fields that are of reasonable sizes. Another field that must be in the header is a sequence number. The packet is then passed to the error detection function which, at the server (sending process), will compute the checksum and place the checksum in the header. The packet is finally sent via the UDP socket to the Web client.
